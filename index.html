How to make, block & measure your gauge
1
Make the swatch in filet stitch
Crochet a swatch at least 20 × 20 cm using your actual yarn and hook. Work in filet mesh: 1 double crochet, chain 2 for open squares, or 3 double crochet for filled squares. Do not use single crochet — filet gauge is completely different.
2
Block it
Wet blocking is essential for filet — the chain spaces shrink 10–20% unblocked. Lightly wet the swatch, lay flat on a foam mat, pin the corners, stretch to a square and pin every 2–3 cm. Let it dry completely overnight before removing pins.
3
Measure once dry
Count how many filet squares fit in 10 cm horizontally (each square = 1 DC + its space) → Stitches per 10 cm. Count rows vertically → Rows per 10 cm. Measure in the centre of the swatch.
Your yarn label may include a reference gauge — useful starting point, but your own blocked swatch is always more accurate.
