How to make, block & measure your gauge
1
Make the swatch in filet stitch
Crochet a swatch at least 20 × 20 cm using your actual yarn and hook. Work in filet mesh: 1 double crochet, chain 2 for open squares, or 3 double crochet for filled squares. Do not use single crochet — filet gauge is completely different.
2
Block it
Wet blocking is essential for filet: the chain spaces are unstable until blocked and can shrink 10–20%. Lightly wet the swatch (spray bottle, or dip & gently squeeze for cotton). Lay it flat on a foam mat or folded towel, pin the corners first, then stretch to a square shape and pin every 2–3 cm along all edges. Let it dry completely before removing pins — overnight is safest.
3
Measure once dry
Lay a ruler flat on the swatch. Count how many filet squares fit in 10 cm horizontally (each square = 1 DC + its space) → enter this as Stitches per 10 cm. Then count how many rows of squares fit in 10 cm vertically → enter as Rows per 10 cm. Measure in the centre of the swatch, away from the edges.
Your yarn label may include a reference gauge — that's a useful starting point, but your own blocked swatch is always more accurate, especially if you crochet tight or loose.
